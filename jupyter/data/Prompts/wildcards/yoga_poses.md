## Mountain Pose
**Sanskrit:** Tadasana

- **Short**: "Mountain Pose (Tadasana), standing tall, feet together, perfect alignment"
- **Description**: Stand with feet together, grounding through all four corners of the feet. Engage the quadriceps, draw the navel toward the spine, and roll the shoulders back while reaching the crown of the head upward to align the entire skeletal system.
- **Image Prompt**: "A calm athletic woman in Mountain Pose (Tadasana) standing tall on a yoga mat in a serene studio, feet together, strong legs engaged, core drawn in, shoulders relaxed down, arms at sides, gaze forward, perfect posture and alignment; side view for full body form, soft natural light."

## Sun Salutation A Sequence
**Sanskrit:** Surya Namaskar A

## Malasana Progressions: The 4 Pillars (Ankle, Calf, Hip, Spine)

**Focus:** Building ankle dorsiflexion, calf elasticity, hip external rotation, and spinal integrity for deep squat mastery.

### 1. Goddess Squat (Wide Stance Sumo Squat)
**Sanskrit:** Utkata Konasana (Goddess Pose—Dynamic Variation from Chandra Namaskar / Moon Salutations)

- **Short**: "Goddess Squat (Utkata Konasana), wide legs, knees tracking over toes, engaging external rotators"
- **Description**: Feet wider than hip-width, toes turned out 45 degrees. Sink hips straight down (avoid anterior pelvic tilt). Knees track over second and third toe. Hands at heart or cactus arms.
- **Image Prompt**: "Powerful woman in Utkata Konasana (Goddess Squat), feet wide apart, knees deeply bent tracking outward over toes, upright torso, hands at heart center or extended; strong external hip rotation, quad engagement visible."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Tight Hip Sockets / Limited External Rotation:** Feet wider, knees less bent, hands on thighs for support, torso more upright, focus on external rotator activation.
- **Moderate Hip Mobility:** Standard wide stance, hands at heart center, squat depth mid-range, core stabilization prominent.
- **Open Hip Sockets / Full External Rotation:** Deeper squat, heel lift optional for balance challenge, hands at heart or extended, femurs fully externally rotated.

**Correction Cues:**
- *Valgus Collapse (knock-knees):* "Press knees toward pinky toes" | *Visual tag:* knees externally rotated, maintaining alignment
- *Anterior Pelvic Tilt (duck butt):* "Drop tailbone straight down like an anchor" | *Visual tag:* neutral pelvis, core engaged, no lumbar arch exaggeration

---

### 2. Side Lunge (War God's Pose)
**Sanskrit:** Skandasana (Pose of the War God Skanda—Vinyasa transition pose)

- **Short**: "Side Lunge (Skandasana), deep adductor stretch, flat-footed grounding"
- **Description**: From standing, step right foot wide to the side. Sink hips toward right heel, keeping left leg straight. Right foot flat, toes forward. Spine vertical or slight forward fold.
- **Image Prompt**: "Athletic woman in deep Skandasana (Side Lunge), right leg bent with foot flat, left leg straight and engaged, torso upright with broad collarbones, hands on ground or at heart; powerful hip adductor opening."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Tight Adductors / High Hip Height:** Hips elevated above knee line, hands on floor or blocks for support, minimal forward lean.
- **Moderate Adductor Length:** Hips sink toward heel level, heel grounded, hands on floor, spine upright or slight forward tilt.
- **Open Adductors / Full Depth:** Deep squat-like position, heel flat, spine vertical, hands to heart center, maximum adductor stretch.

**Correction Cues:**
- *Rounded Upper Back:* "Lift sternum to sky, broaden collarbones" | *Visual tag:* chest open, shoulders back, straight spine
- *Hyperextended Straight Leg Knee:* "Micro-bend the straight leg's knee" | *Visual tag:* engaged quadriceps, slight knee bend to protect joint

### 3. Wide-Legged Forward Fold to Squat
**Sanskrit:** Prasarita Padottanasana → Malasana (compression-release principle from Ashtanga yoga)

- **Short**: "Wide Fold to Squat (Prasarita → Malasana), alternating hamstring stretch and hip opener"
- **Description**: Start in wide-legged forward fold (legs straight, chest toward floor). Bend knees deeply, sinking hips into wide squat. Return to fold, straightening legs. Repeat dynamically.
- **Image Prompt**: "Woman flowing between Prasarita Padottanasana (wide fold, straight legs, hands on ground) and deep squat position with bent knees and hips low; demonstrating spinal integrity by maintaining flat back in fold phase."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Limited Hamstring Length / Spinal Sensitivity:** Hands on blocks in fold, generous knee bend throughout, belly approaching thighs, focus on maintaining neutral spine.
- **Moderate Hamstring Elasticity:** Hands on floor with bent knees in fold phase, progression toward straighter legs as mobility improves.
- **Full Hamstring Extension / Spinal Integrity:** Hands to floor, legs straight in fold phase, flat back maintained, dynamic pulses into and out of squat.

**Correction Cues:**
- *Spinal Flexion/Rounding:* "Lead with your chest, not your head" | *Visual tag:* flat spine, long neutral back
- *Weight in Toes/Balls of Feet:* "Shift hips backward, trust heels" | *Visual tag:* heel grounding, even weight distribution

### 4. Parallel Forward Fold to Squat
**Sanskrit:** Uttanasana → Utkatasana variation (deconstructing Surya Namaskar B transitions)

- **Short**: "Forward Fold to Squat (Uttanasana → Utkatasana), posterior chain stretch to core activation"
- **Description**: Forward fold with feet parallel and hip-width apart. Bend knees, drop hips toward heels (deep squat prep). Return to fold. Emphasize Manipura (solar plexus/belly) to thigh connection.
- **Image Prompt**: "Woman alternating between deep Uttanasana (forward fold, straight legs) and low squat position with hips dropped, maintaining core-to-thigh awareness; side view showing spine elongation in fold."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Limited Ankle Dorsiflexion / Tight Calves:** Hold squat position briefly, let heels lift slightly if needed, hands on mat or blocks, focus on ankle opening.
- **Moderate Ankle Mobility:** Smooth transitions between fold and squat, heels grounded or nearly grounded, hands on floor for balance.
- **Full Ankle Dorsiflexion / Strong Mobility:** Deep fold with straight legs, heels planted in squat, dynamic flow between positions, advanced control.

### 5. Kneeling Ankle Floss (Toe Squat & Thunderbolt Variations)
**Sanskrit:** Vajrasana (Thunderbolt Pose)—Yin variation with toes tucked

- **Short**: "Ankle Floss (Vajrasana variants), dorsiflexion work for Malasana prep"
- **Description**: Kneeling on mat. Oscillate between toes tucked underneath (Toe Squat—Yin) and toes extended flat (Vajrasana—Hatha). Targets plantar fascia, toe meridian lines, and ankle mobility.
- **Image Prompt**: "Woman in kneeling position, alternating between toes tucked underneath (intense dorsiflexion, Yin Toe Squat) and toes relaxed flat (Vajrasana); close-up showing ankle and foot engagement, circulation flush."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Limited Ankle / Toe Tolerance:** Knees on mat, toes tucked, upright torso, minimal or no body weight into toes, brief holds only.
- **Moderate Ankle Mobility / Building Tolerance:** Slowly shift body weight back toward heels, resting gently on tucked toes, oscillate between variations at manageable intensity.
- **Open Ankles / Full Comfort:** Advanced weight distribution into toes, extended holds in tucked position, smooth oscillation between Toe Squat (Yin) and Vajrasana (Hatha), active ankle motor control.

**Correction Cues:**
- *Immediate Toe Cramping/Resistance:* "Hold light weight distribution; build tolerance gradually" | *Visual tag:* controlled descent, measured intensity
- *Ankle Inversion (rolling outward):* "Press inner arch of foot into mat" | *Visual tag:* foot neutral alignment, arches engaged

### 6. Low Lunge Knee Drives (Ankle Dorsiflexion Work)
**Sanskrit:** Anjaneyasana (Low Lunge—Modification emphasizing front ankle)

- **Short**: "Low Lunge Knee Drives (Anjaneyasana var.), dorsiflexion and front ankle engagement"
- **Description**: Low lunge position with back knee on mat. Instead of backbend, lean weight forward to increase front ankle dorsiflexion (closing ankle angle). Feel stretch along top of foot and anterior shin.
- **Image Prompt**: "Athletic woman in deep Low Lunge (Anjaneyasana), back knee on mat, body leaned forward, front foot showing strong dorsiflexion (heel planted, toes drawn toward shin); intense ankle joint opening."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Limited Ankle Dorsiflexion / Sensitive Ankle:** Hands on floor, gently rock forward until heel wants to lift, maintain static hold, light intensity.
- **Moderate Dorsiflexion / Progressive Range:** Forearm on front thigh, lean body weight to passively deepen dorsiflexion, dynamic holds.
- **Full Dorsiflexion / Advanced Engagement:** While leaned forward, actively pull toes toward shin (Tibialis Anterior activation), deepens self-stretch, high intensity.

**Correction Cues:**
- *Heel Pops Off Mat:* "Imagine heel glued to mat; work within comfortable range" | *Visual tag:* heel grounded, controlled dorsiflexion
- *Inward Knee Collapse:* "Track knee over second and third toe" | *Visual tag:* knee alignment, no valgus stress

### 8. Downward-Facing Dog with Pedals & Soleus Drops
**Sanskrit:** Adho Mukha Svanasana (with Gastrocnemius & Soleus-focused variations)

- **Short**: "Downward Dog Pedals & Drops (Adho Mukha Svanasana), calf differentiation work"
- **Description**: In Downward Dog, alternate "walking the dog" (pedaling heels down one at a time—Gastrocnemius focus) with "double knee bend" (bending both knees, heels lift, isolating Soleus). Builds ankle range for Malasana.
- **Image Prompt**: "Athletic woman in Adho Mukha Svanasana, demonstrating both phases: (1) alternating heel drops (pedaling), and (2) double knee bend with heels lifted; posterior chain and calf differentiation work, side view."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Tight Calves / Limited Ankle Range:** Both knees bent, focus on flat spine from wrists to hips, heels lifted, passive holds, minimal load into feet.
- **Moderate Calf Elasticity / Progressive Loading:** Alternating heel drops (pedaling), smooth transitions, moderate intensity.
- **Full Calf Extension / Open Ankles:** Legs straight, heels reaching toward or touching mat, sustained holds in both pedaling and straight-leg phases, advanced control.

**Correction Cues:**
- *"Turtle Shell" (Hunched Shoulders):* "Wrap triceps back, create space between ears and shoulders" | *Visual tag:* shoulders open, engaged scapulae
- *Rounded Lower Back (Lumbar Flexion):* "Lift sit bones high and wide, bend knees until spine is flat" | *Visual tag:* neutral spine, extended posterior chain

### 9. Garland Pose (Malasana)—Peak Pose
**Sanskrit:** Malasana (Garland Pose)—integration of all four pillars

- **Short**: "Garland Pose (Malasana), deep squat, elbows pressing inner knees, heels grounded"
- **Description**: Deep squat with feet wider than hip-width, toes turned out 45 degrees. Heels down (or on blanket if needed). Elbows press inner knees outward. Hands in Anjali Mudra (prayer position) at heart. Spine lengthens upward while rooting tailbone. Grounding through all four corners of feet (Pada Bandha—foot lock).
- **Image Prompt**: "Serene woman in deep Malasana (Garland Pose), heels grounded, wide stance, knees externally rotated with elbows pressing inward, hands in prayer at heart center, upright torso, calm grounded expression; front view emphasizing hip opening and spinal neutrality."

**Anatomical Variations:**
- **Limited Mobility / Prop-Assisted (Block):** Sit on yoga block; removes muscular effort, allows gravity-assisted hip opening without strain.
- **Moderate Mobility / Counter-Balance Assist:** Hold 10lb weight plate or heavy book with straight arms extended forward; counter-balance prevents falling backward, enables deeper squat.
- **Moderate-to-Advanced Mobility / Heel Support:** Roll blanket under heels, creating "fake floor" for tight ankles; maintains squat depth and structural integrity of spine.
- **Full Expression / No Props:** Heels down, spine tall, hands at heart, no props; elbows press knees, knees press back (isometric tension), complete integration.

**Correction Cues:**
- *Collapsed Chest (Slouching):* "Press thumbs into sternum, lift chest to meet them" | *Visual tag:* open heart, upright torso, elongated spine
- *Knees Caving Inward (Against Elbows):* "Squeeze glutes to drive knees open" or "Isometric: press elbows into knees, knees back into elbows" | *Visual tag:* external hip rotation, engaged glutes
- *Collapsed Arches (Rolling Onto Inner Foot):* "Lift inner arches, press outer edges of feet" (Pada Bandha / Foot Lock) | *Visual tag:* arches engaged, weight distributed through heel and outer foot edge
